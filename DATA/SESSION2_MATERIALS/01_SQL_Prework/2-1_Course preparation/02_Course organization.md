# Course organization

Type: Article

The course is divided into 5 components:

1. Prework - or course preparation,
2. Session 1 — `SQL` basics,
3. Session 2 — Data analysis and Procedural Language for `SQL`,
4. Exam
5. Final project — to complete on your own.

## Prework

During prework you will learn the basics of `SQL`, configure the database connection and learn about the history of relational databases. What's more, as a supplement you will learn the basics of `UML` and get a foundation in `NoSQL` databases.

## Session 1

During this weekend you will learn, among other things, how to manipulate datasets - how to add, modify or delete data from a table. In addition, we will discuss the basics of logic, which directly applies to `SQL` queries. Later on we will talk about relationships and their types, as well as the types of connecting tables (`joins`).

## Session 2

This session is devoted to data analysis and `SQL` procedural language. Among others, we will talk about aggregation methods and advanced analytical tools such as **window function**. The concepts of cursors, transactions and other additional, often useful functions, will also be introduced.

## Exam

At the end of the course you are going to take an exam. It will be a test held on the last day of classes at 9:00am (it takes 45 minutes). Passing the test with a minimum score of 50% is required to complete the course. One re-take is allowed.

## Final project

The final project is based on a different database than the one discussed in the course and is designed to present common tasks for a data analyst - from row selection, to report preparation, to making recommendations.
