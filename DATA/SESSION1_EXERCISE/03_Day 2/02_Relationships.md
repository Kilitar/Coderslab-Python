## Relationships

See the full schema of the `Sakila` database relationships.

![db-schema](/presentations/DTL/en/4.9/W/M_03_S_02/f5dde102-13e2-492e-b616-ed61cad14c86/exercises/5fd5e5d7-b7a6-4a7c-9a34-79ddeebeaac8/./images/sakila-db-with-views.png)

Determine the types of relationships between:

- `staff` and `rental` - you will do this exercise together with the lecturer,
- `store` and `film`,
- `city` and `country`,
- `language` and `film`,
- `actor` and `film`,
- `customer` and `store`.

It is not visible in the schema but try to use simple `SQL` queries to also determine which columns take part in the relationships.

If you discover that the relationship between two tables is n-n, also give the name of the intermediate (junction) table between them.
