### Presenting the problem

Getting to the point - this time, we'll be analyzing data from the automotive sector. We'd like to learn a little more about the cars that were sold in the United States in 2019. In the dataset, we have a list of cars that are described by as many as 31 different parameters.

Due to the large number of variables, for a better understanding of what we need to do we should start by explaining the meaning of each variable:

- `car_id` – car identifier
- `manufacturer` – car producer
- `model_year` – model launch year
- `category` – car type
- `msrp` – manufacturer's suggested retail price (in dollars)
- `mpg` – average fuel consumption, in miles per gallon
- `engine` – type of engine
- `drivetrain` – type of drive (front, rear...)
- `passenger_capacity` – maximum number of passengers
- `passenger_doors` – number of doors
- `base_curb_weight` – total vehicle mass
- `wheelbase` – axle distance (between the center of the front and rear axle wheels)
- `height` – total height of car (in inches)
- `fuel_tank_capacity` – capacity of fuel tank (in gallons)
- `net_torque` – optimum torque
- `net_horsepower` – power generated at the crankshaft of the engine (without drive losses)
- `displacement` – engine displacement
- `trans_type` – type of gear transmission
- `brakes_abs` – determines whether the car is equipped with ABS
- `front_suspension` – type of front suspension used
- `rear_suspension` – type of rear suspension used
- `traction_control` – determines whether the car is equipped with traction control system
- `parking_aid` – determines whether the car is equipped with parking aid sensors
- `tire_pressure_monitor` – determines whether the car is equipped with tire pressure sensors
- `backup_camera` – determines whether the car is equipped with a backup camera
- `stability_control` – determines whether the car is equipped with track stabilization system
- `basic_years` – number of years under warranty
- `turning_diameter` – turn diameter (in feet)
- `front_tire_width` – width of front tires (in millimeters)
- `front_tire_rim_size` – width of front rims (in inches)

### Define the problem, i.e. what do we actually want to find out?

OK, we already know what type of data each column in our collection stores. Now it's time to define the problem, that is, what we want to find out about the cars in our database. First of all, we want to know:

1. How many cars were offered in 2019?
2. How many cars are there that were offered for sale by age (i.e., the difference between the year of sale and the model year)? How many cars are offered that are older than 10 years?
3. How many cars does each manufacturer sell?
4. Do manufacturers produce more cars with large engines, or more with smaller engines? Is there a relationship here between engine displacement and the model year of the car?
5. What is the relationship between average fuel consumption and engine displacement? Which car has the largest engine displacement? What is its average combustion? Which car is the most economical?
6. Does the price of a car depend on its horsepower? What horsepower does the most powerful car offered have?
7. Is there a relationship between the range of the car and its total weight? The air resistance that the car can put up (hint: it may depend on the height of the car)?

We have asked some questions that will give us a better understanding of what cars sold in 2019. Nevertheless, we greatly appreciate your creativity. It's possible that after reviewing the data you'll come up with other interesting ideas that will expand our knowledge of the cars sold. We hope you'll share your insights with us and include answers to the questions you've managed to define.
