# Big Data

Type: Article

### Introduction

`Big Data` is a catchphrase that gains popularity across a variety of places: Internet, media and, most of all, in companies.
In fact, the term appears in so many contexts that, for good measure, it's not entirely clear exactly what it means.

What is certain is that Big Data is growing by the day.

It is estimated that about 90% of the world's digital data has been collected in the last few years alone. However, let's try to be a little more precise about what Big Data is.

### Big Data - definition

Big Data - this is a relatively new concept and represents the growing amount of data, which is characterized by great variety.
More and more information is passed across the web, which means that it can be used by people working with data as a source of data that can be processed and extract specific information from it.

Data is collected everywhere and about everything - e.g. social media, streaming apps (both audio and video).
Plenty of data is also collected in IoT (*Internet of Things*) sensors.

In general, everything we do online is stored as data. Reading a book on a Kindle reader, for example, generates data about what we read.
Our smartphones also collect data - about our location, how fast we move, the apps we use and how long we use them.

### "V" as a letter describing Big Data features

As we mentioned, Big Data is not only defined by the amount of data, but also by the fact that data in various formats falls under this label - audio, video, sensor readings, app logs, transactional data or simply text data. There are several words describing Big Data starting with the letter "V," so we can call it the "four Vs" principle:

- Volume (*amount*) - the word means more than just the intensity of sound - it is also used to describe the amount or capacity - it quantifies the "big" in "Big Data". The thriving growth of the Internet, social media, smart home solutions and mobile devices has caused the volume of data to expand incredibly (and keeps expanding every minute).
- Velocity (*speed*) - in addition to the increase in volume, engineers are constantly working on solutions to process the vast amount of information as quickly as possible
- Variety (*diversity*) - data once collected in tables in databases is now also collected in many unstructured formats, such as server logs, geographic coordinates, photos, audio or video
- Variability (*ambiguity*) - the meaning of words in unstructured data can be different according to context

### Roles and sample uses of Big Data in business

Big Data is important not only because of the amount of data, but also in terms of the type of data and how we can potentially use it to benefit the company we work for.

Big Data allows us to accurately analyze product development and make business decisions. Combined with analytics, we can infer:

- the causes of successes and failures in the company,
- the sales trends based on historical customer purchases
- whether users are engaging in dishonest behavior
- how we can reduce the risks that affect the organization

As the technology allows for increased efficiency and the development of new data-driven services, Big Data is finding many applications in various fields:

- social media and advertising
- anomaly detection (e.g., fraud)
- weather forecasting
- healthcare
- online stores

#### Social media and advertising

One widely used use case for Big Data is the data collected through interactions with various social networks, such as Facebook, Instagram and Twitter.

Data is collected in the form of comments, reactions to posts/comments or images.

Companies can use the data to better understand customer requirements and see how users are expressing themselves on social media.
By doing so, they build strategies that can benefit the company's future growth.

#### Finding anomalies

Detecting anomalies, and thus preventing fraud, is one of the most common uses of Big Data.
Collecting financial data in huge volumes about customers, accounts and individual transactions allows it to be used to detect and prevent crime.

In the past, payment card operators tracked all transactions and if a suspicious event occurred, the buyer was directly contacted and confirmed that the transaction had been completed.

Today, models that detect purchase patterns monitor user behavior and automatically detect fraud.

#### Weather forecasting

Another application is the ability to predict the weather.

A plethora of climate information is currently being collected.

Collecting and analyzing this type of data can be useful for predicting short- and long-term weather phenomena, such as floods or hurricanes, which can, for example, save the lives of people who live in areas at high risk of the phenomenon.

#### Healthcare

Big Data also has its merits in the context of healthcare.

The buoyant growth of medicine has caused the medical industry to also collect large amounts of patient data.
Historical data (e.g., test results or medical interviews) can be used to analyze patients' health status and prevent future diseases.

Another fairly recent example is the COVID-19 pandemic.

At one point, there was a lot of buzz about smartphone operating system manufacturers introducing mechanisms to notify the users of potential contact with an infected person.

Phones track huge amounts of data about users' locations, which are further processed by algorithms for various purposes. In the past, Google has attempted to accurately predict flu activity based on flu-related searches. Of course, knowing the user's location, it was possible, for example, to estimate how many people could potentially be sick in the selected area.

#### Online stores

More and more people are changing the way they shop (which the pandemic has also contributed to). They are more willing to shop online, resulting in increased activity on the websites of various online stores.

Users visiting online stores are interacting with them a lot, in search of the products they are most interested in at the moment. The vast amount of data about what a given user displayed, what they bought, what they added to the shopping cart, but ultimately abandoned... allows the construction of recommendation engines that, based on historical actions, can suggest to the user what they should buy during the current visit to a virtual store.

One of the more interesting solutions is a kind of clustering (*collaborative filtering*), which is based on the idea of community and, for example, finds similar users to you based on the interactions you have made on the site and recommends relevant products to you (it could be, for example, a movie on Netflix, a piece of music on Spotify or a smartphone to buy on eBay).

### Key Big Data terms worth knowing

The development of technology also brings with it the creation of many new words, the meaning of which may initially be unintuitive and unfamiliar to you. Therefore, we have prepared a compilation of some popular terms closely related to Big Data:

- **algorithm** - a finite sequence of defined steps that are necessary to complete a task.
- **cloud computing** - running software on remote servers that are interconnected and process huge amounts of data quickly and efficiently, which isn't possible using, for example, a local workstation with limited hardware resources
- **predictive analytics** - the use of analytics to predict trends or future events.
- **structured and unstructured data** - structured data can be placed in a table in an organized manner; unstructured data is other data that, for example, must be stored as separate files
- The **Internet of Things** (*IoT*) - refers to objects (e.g., detectors) that collect, analyze and transmit their own data in an automated manner.
- **Spark** - a technology that allows the storage, retrieval and analysis of huge data sets, using distributed computing technology. By default, Spark processes data in memory (rather than on disk), making computation significantly faster.

### Summary

In this article, we learned what Big Data really is.

We saw examples of applications of data that are collected in huge amounts.

The popularity of building solutions based on huge data sets is growing rapidly.

In the course of your data analysis journey, sooner or later you will come into direct contact with this technology, which is, of course, a great prospect for the future.
