## Introduction and summarizing the stage of framing the problem

Together, we repeated the issue of defining the problem in the process of data analysis. After the first portion of the answers, it turned out that not everything is clear yet and we need to fill the gaps in our knowledge.

We have found out that:

- The offered rental units are located in New York (USA).
- The room price is quoted in US dollars and is for one hotel night
- The number of reviews refers to a specific offer
- Data comes from the period of 07.09.2021 – 07.09.2022
- No marketing activities were carried out
- In the `availability_365` column there is data about the number of days in the following 365 when the room is going to be available (e.g. the value of 200 means that in the next 365 days the room is available for a maximum of 200 days). Availability may be limited because the property has been booked by other guests or blocked by the host for a specific period of time.

## Obtaining data – where can data come from?

Let's not waste time and go to the next issue: collecting the data for analysis. Working as a data analyst, you're bound to come across data that comes from many different sources. The most common places and ways that you'll use to obtain data are:

- Databases (e.g., SQL-based) that are created and maintained by your company's data engineers
- External database
- Public datasets shared as files (e.g., `.csv` or `.xls`)
- API – application programming interface
- Web scraping — extracting data from websites
