# Course organization

Type: Article

You must be consumed with curiosity about what we'll actually cover in the "Introduction to Data Analysis" course. Below you'll find our course schedule:

1. **Prework**
  
  Preparing for the course, setting up the environment and getting acquainted with the process of analyzing data using visual examples
2. **Session 1**
  
  Working with data in Excel (data collection, cleaning and processing, exploration and in-depth analysis).
3. **Homework**
  
  Exercises you do on your own using the knowledge gained during the first session on a new dataset.
4. **Session 2**
  
  Communicating results (creating visualizations in Excel), introduction to A/B testing
5. **Final project**
  
  Developed independently during the last day of the course.

Both sessions are held on the weekend - with a two-week break. Between the sessions you'll gain knowledge on your own - on the LMS platform you'll find materials to work through on your own. These will be both additional materials to expand the knowledge from the session just completed, and assignments that will allow you to consolidate the knowledge you have gained. As part of the final session, you do a final project in which you'll conduct real-world data analysis on your own.

## Prework

During the *prework* you'll learn about the data analysis process with a discussion of its elements; you'll also install and configure Microsoft Excel 365. Additionally, we're verifying that you already have basic Excel skills.

Through **case study** you'll learn how the steps in the data analysis process look like in practice. After each step, you're going to perform a simple task that will verify your knowledge of Excel and help you develop the right habits in working with data.

At the end of *prework* you'll get familiar with the `Kaggle` platform, that will let you develop your data analyst skills thanks to its huge collection of datasets, and large community of users who readily share their knowledge and experience in working with data.

** To be admitted to the first class with the lecturer, you need to pass the prework at a level of 80%!** Prework must be passed before the start of the first class with the lecturer.

**Remember to systematically complete the prework material. Start working independently as soon as possible to better acquire the material that is new to you.

**Pro tip: Use the **"Done" and "Read "** button at the very bottom of each material to monitor your progress in completing tasks and reading articles and presentations.

## Session 1

During the first weekend you'll learn the methods of working with data in Excel 365 and complete the first steps of the data analysis process. You will deepen your knowledge with issues related to loading, cleaning up data errors and preliminary understanding of data on multiple levels, i.e. *exploration*. For this, you're going to use descriptive statistics and selected visualizations.
After the first session, you'll have homework to do involving the exploration of a new dataset.

## Session 2

During the second weekend you are going to learn how to effectively communicate the results of data analysis. You will also gain knowledge of A/B testing, which is increasingly in demand in the market for data analysts.

The second session concludes with a full-day final project, during which you'll conduct a complete data analysis on your own.

## Final project

The final project will be done during the last day of class. The project will be based on a new dataset. In this project you will have a chance to integrate the knowledge and skills you have acquired. You will independently go through the entire process of data analysis: from understanding, cleaning and exploring the data through analyzing to presenting the results and the story (*story telling*) contained in the developed data sets.
